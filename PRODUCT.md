# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Prospective residential and commercial clients in Sweden and internationally who are looking for interior architecture, visualization, or design consultation. Visitors need to understand the studio's character, assess the quality and relevance of its work, learn how it approaches projects, and confidently begin an enquiry. The site must serve both Swedish- and English-speaking audiences. A secondary audience includes design peers, creative developers, editors, award juries, and the wider architecture and digital-design community.

## Product Purpose

Present Lagom Studio as a distinctive Malmö-based interior architecture practice, communicate its point of view through completed work, and convert suitable visitors into thoughtful project enquiries. The website should preserve the substance of the existing site while replacing its conventional portfolio presentation with a more cinematic, memorable, and spatial experience. It is also intended as a showcase-level digital work suitable for submission to Awwwards and consideration by the GSAP community across their websites and social channels. Recognition is an explicit ambition, but it must result from original art direction, technical craft, performance, accessibility, and a coherent story rather than spectacle alone.

## Brand Personality

Bold, smooth, clean, precise, and quietly confident. Minimalism should create focus rather than anonymity. Large typography, strong image composition, and controlled motion give the studio a recognizable character while the voice remains direct, human, and composed.

## Anti-references

- Do not copy OH Architecture, Yazdani Studio, or any other reference site directly; borrow principles of pacing, image choreography, and spatial transition only.
- Avoid generic beige luxury, templated architecture portfolios, sterile minimalism, corporate real-estate language, and decoration without purpose.
- Avoid playful or trend-led effects that compete with the projects.
- Avoid constant scroll effects, excessive page-load delays, and motion that makes the site feel like a showreel instead of a working studio website.
- Avoid familiar “award-site” tricks used without narrative purpose, including gratuitous WebGL, illegible experimental navigation, cursor gimmicks, scroll hijacking, and transitions that delay access to content.
- The current site's content is authoritative context, but its conventional grid-and-section presentation is not the target visual direction.

## Design Principles

1. Let the work carry the brand: project imagery is the primary material, not decoration around it.
2. Give each movement a spatial reason: transitions should reveal, connect, or reframe content rather than merely animate it.
3. Be bold through scale and composition: use strong typography and decisive layouts while keeping the interface clean.
4. Preserve calm under motion: choreograph a small number of memorable sequences and keep ordinary reading frictionless.
5. Build one bilingual system: Swedish and English should have equal structure, quality, and editorial care.
6. Make recognition a consequence of craft: every showcase moment must strengthen Lagom's story, remain smooth on real devices, and still work without motion.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. Support keyboard navigation, visible focus states, semantic landmarks, descriptive alternative text, sufficient contrast, and layouts resilient to longer Swedish and English copy. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` with immediate or simplified alternatives for every animation, avoid motion-dependent meaning, and never block access to content behind an intro sequence.
