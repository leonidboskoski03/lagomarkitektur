# Visual Direction Bible

This document owns the permanent appearance of the film. Sequence files describe actions, not new art direction.

## North star

The final villa should feel latent inside the folded drawing from the beginning. The visual progression is:

> mineral stillness → precise black line → inhabitable volume → restrained material → complete place

The result is premium through proportion, light, restraint, and material truth—not through decorative luxury or visual effects.

## Character

The film is:

- Calm, precise, tactile, architectural, daylight-led, and contemporary.
- Warm enough to feel inhabited, but never yellow or sentimental.
- Scandinavian in restraint, not a catalogue of design clichés.
- Cinematic without behaving like a fantasy portal or product reveal.

The film is not:

- Blue paper with white blueprint lines.
- A generic CAD interface.
- A construction timelapse.
- Sci-fi, holographic, particle-heavy, or liquid-metal.
- A glossy luxury advertisement.
- A copy of the supplied process references.

## Opening studio world

The table is uncluttered rather than empty. The folded drawing is always the visual center.

Approved opening object set:

- Folded white architectural plan.
- One thin pale-limestone façade sample.
- One thin light-oak veneer sample.
- One recognizable slim matte-black architectural ruler with restrained tick marks and no generated text.
- One black mechanical pencil.

The closed packet is approximately A3 (`297 × 420 mm`), representing an A1 sheet (`594 × 841 mm`) folded twice. It should be roughly 1.6–1.8 times larger than the first K00 exploration and remain the obvious protagonist.

Maximum four supporting objects. Keep them near frame edges. No linen/textile swatch, cup, laptop, hands, glasses, books, plant, coasters, thick sample blocks, or generic architect props.

### K00 directions to test

1. **Stone:** pale honed limestone table with warm gray mineral floor.
2. **Mineral:** off-white mineral-composite table with slightly darker microcement floor.
3. **Oak:** very light oak table with neutral mineral floor; reject if it feels domestic rather than studio-like.

All variables except the table material should remain identical between directions.

## Paper and drawing language

- The physical sheet is white with black ink.
- The folded outer face is intentionally blank in K00, K01, and K02. The drawing is printed on the concealed inner face and first becomes visible when K03 opens the horizontal fold.
- The blank outer face still shows restrained paper fiber, fold relief, thin folded edges, and a natural contact shadow so it reads as a folded architectural sheet rather than an empty card.
- K03 reveals the registered upper A2 half of the portrait A1 plan master after the left-right fold opens; K04 reveals the lower half when the perpendicular top-bottom fold opens.
- In K03/K04, similarity to the supplied drawing is judged first by room order, walls, doors, and window/opening positions. Furniture marks may be visually simplified for line clarity at this scale; later spatial furniture footprints remain governed by the source lock.
- The canonical drawing is rotated 90° counterclockwise for the film presentation, placing the outdoor courtyard/site area on the right. K03/K04 reveal allocation must be derived from this single rotated master rather than rotating independently generated frames.
- Plan ink is composited as a transparent neutral multiply layer so the physical paper's fiber, fold shading, and daylight remain continuous beneath every line; no rectangular source-paper patch or foreign shadow may remain.
- Plan line hierarchy: structural walls and primary boundaries are black; doors/windows are clearly legible; furniture and sanitary symbols use stronger neutral gray. The bottom-left bed/sofa room, its adjacent flexible room, living furniture, and courtyard seating must remain readable at the K03 camera scale.
- Courtyard paving/hatch is subordinate to the furniture, planting, and architectural boundaries. Strengthen meaningful objects rather than allowing repetitive hatch texture to dominate.
- In the rotated film orientation, the living room is the top-center room. Its canonical L-shaped sofa runs along the room's top edge and turns down the right edge; this furniture anchor must remain visible in K03 and all later spatial states.
- Fold geometry is independent from plan geometry.
- Drawing lines meet exactly across fold boundaries.
- Physical creases, edge curl, contact shadows, and fiber relief exist only before K05.
- Red axes and green landscape hatches from the source are restyled or separated; they do not remain red/green.
- Primary walls use the strongest black line.
- Furniture and dimension lines use lighter hierarchy while remaining black/gray.
- Text generated by AI is never accepted; exact annotations are composited from controlled assets.

## Interior proposal

The interior is a proposed visualization until confirmed otherwise.

### Global rules

- Light-oak floor in dry spaces.
- Matte warm-white walls and ceilings.
- Pale stone at kitchen and bathrooms.
- Light-gray woven textiles.
- Natural linen curtains at real openings only.
- Matte-black minimal lighting and details.
- Abundant natural daylight.
- Low-profile furniture.
- No added furniture outside approved footprints.
- K09 must show material progress in every visible room. Its intermediate state is measured by incomplete secondary surfaces/details across the model, not by leaving the bedroom or bathroom as entirely untextured white-clay rooms.
- Every real exterior window opening visible in K09 and later must contain readable clear glazing. Use nearly colorless low-reflection glass with only a faint cool-neutral edge/highlight and slim dark framing; never leave a window as an empty gap or use saturated blue/green, mirrored, frosted, or opaque glass.

### Primary bedroom

- Bed stays centered in its drawn footprint.
- Low light-oak headboard or integrated oak detail.
- Pale gray linen bedding with white layers.
- East storage remains fixed.
- No additional bench, chair, or decorative plant without footprint approval.

### Office / guest room

- Preserve both desks and both task chairs along the south opening.
- Thin light-oak worktops and quiet storage.
- Preserve the west daybed/sofa arrangement.
- Black task lights may articulate each work position.
- Preserve the broad clear terrace-facing window in the screen-right wall beside the worktable; it spans the work positions while retaining short structural wall piers and remains distinct from the separate balcony glass balustrade.

### Entry and circulation

- Treat the central spine as the spatial organizer.
- Use pale stone or light oak at the threshold.
- Do not use an orange glowing doorway or fantasy light source.

### Kitchen

- Preserve perimeter layout, appliances, cooktop, and circulation.
- Warm-white cabinetry with selective light-oak fronts.
- Pale stone worktop/backsplash.
- No added island unless the architect confirms one.

### Bathroom

- Preserve basin and sanitary footprints.
- Warm-white/pale-stone large surfaces.
- Minimal black fittings.
- No reconfigured shower or freestanding bath without technical confirmation.

### Living and dining

- Dining remains in the northern portion and lounge in the southern portion.
- Light-oak dining table and slim chairs within the drawn footprint.
- Pale-gray lounge upholstery.
- Preserve sofa, lounge chairs, small tables, openings, and terrace relationship.
- Preserve the broad courtyard-facing living glazing across most of its real exterior wall.
- The kitchen and living/dining zone read as one open-plan volume; do not turn plan separators or furniture edges into walls.
- This room carries the strongest material-growth passage.

### Exterior terraces and garden

- Pale mineral paving.
- Low architectural planting.
- Preserve both seating areas.
- Keep the large courtyard presentation edge completely open.
- Use a slim transparent glass balustrade with minimal dark framing at the small raised terrace only; never replace it with an opaque wall.
- Stop the small-terrace balustrade where the usable balcony platform ends; do not continue glass beyond the dotted grass/planting strip.
- No pool, pergola, firepit, outdoor kitchen, or added furniture.

### K07 white-clay quality lock

- Use `K07_v001_white-clay-raised-wireframe.png` as the rendering-quality reference only: smooth neutral architectural white, clean matte clay, restrained soft-gray side shading, crisp fine graphite edges, and clear pale plan linework.
- Later geometry controls the building; v001 must never restore superseded walls, openings, room arrangements, or courtyard boundaries.
- Reject cumulative pink/beige mottling, cloudy texture, haze, grain, blurred edges, low-contrast furniture, and the softened appearance caused by repeated whole-frame generative edits.
- When edit accumulation degrades the frame, rebuild once from the latest geometry plus the v001 quality reference instead of continuing to stack edits.
- `K07_v030_user-approved-clean-wireframe.png` supersedes all earlier K07 visual candidates and is the approved color, clarity, line-weight, camera, shell, site, and white-clay boundary for K08.

## Lighting system

### Primary daylight version

- Large soft source from upper left in the studio.
- Cooler, restrained fill from the opposite side.
- Real contact shadows.
- Stable exposure and white balance across clips.
- Exterior final grade follows the supplied renders.

### Integrated lighting

The project description identifies integrated linear lighting as important. Use it as:

- A subtle architectural detail when the exterior envelope completes, or
- A separate optional dusk ending after the primary daylight film is approved.

Do not introduce a dramatic day-to-night transition into the first master.

## Material-growth appearance

- Material travels as a surface-bound capillary front.
- The leading edge is soft and slightly irregular.
- A small wet highlight may exist only at the leading edge.
- The surface behind it dries immediately to matte.
- The effect follows UV/surface planes and never crosses open air.
- Geometry never melts or changes.
- Material families resolve in order: oak, white, stone, textile, black detail.

## Final exterior

Visualization 1 is the recommended K13 anchor because it shows the most complete relationship among stone, entrance, ivory panel volume, glazing, terraces, and planting. Final selection remains open.

The last frame should use the approved real render or a minimally extended/composited version. It should not be independently regenerated by AI.

## Permanent prohibitions

- Blue blueprint paper or white ink.
- Extra walls, doors, windows, rooms, or furniture.
- Fisheye or extreme wide-angle distortion.
- Orange portal light, particles, magical glow, or holograms.
- Heavy bloom, deep crushed shadows, or oversaturation.
- Glossy marble, brass-heavy luxury, dark walnut, or excessive boucle.
- People, hands, workers, or moving props.
- Generated text or distorted logos.
- Texture boiling, exposure pumping, or color-temperature drift.
